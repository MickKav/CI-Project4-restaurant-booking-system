Slow-cooked to perfection Lamb Stew

This absolute succulence of a dish is slow-cooked for 3 hours and delivered in a rustic cast iron pot. Seasoned with local herbs and spices it is ideally accompanied by a pint of stout of your choice.
The origin of the Irish stew is believed to have been roughly the 17th century. Back then, the dish was often made by shepherds and rural farmers who had access to only a few ingredients but needed a nourishing meal to sustain them through long days of work.

Deliciously creamy Seafood Chowder

We source our ingredients from local fisherman while keeping a ratio of smoked fish and diced bacon to create the special flavour and a range of shellfish and prawns to add to the juicy experience.

Ireland's own pancake Boxty

Boxty is an authentic Irish dish which originated in the 1700s. It's made from potatoesand is great for breakfast, brunch or whenever you feel. It was a staple food in Irish households where potatoes were relied upon to survive. Blight during the famine destroyed the potato crop and affected the consumption of Boxty.
